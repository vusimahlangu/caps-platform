Mechanics (Paper 1)

Law: F = ma

Example: Find the force on a 2 kg object accelerating at 3 m·s⁻².

Solution: F = 2 × 3 = 6 N

Common Mistake: Using mass in grams instead of kilograms.

Practice: Calculate acceleration if F = 10 N and m = 2 kg.

Waves, Sound & Light (Paper 1)

Formula: v = fλ

Example: Find the wave speed if f = 5 Hz and λ = 2 m.

Solution: v = 10 m·s⁻¹

Common Mistake: Mixing up frequency and wavelength.

Practice: Find λ if v = 20 m·s⁻¹ and f = 4 Hz.

Electricity & Magnetism (Paper 1)

Formula: V = IR

Example: Find the voltage if I = 2 A and R = 5 Ω.

Solution: V = 10 V

Common Mistake: Confusing current and voltage.

Practice: Calculate current if V = 12 V and R = 6 Ω.

Matter & Materials (Paper 2)

Concept: Intermolecular forces affect boiling point.

Example: Why does water have a high boiling point?

Answer: Strong hydrogen bonding.

Common Mistake: Confusing intermolecular and intramolecular forces.

Practice: Name the IMF in HCl.

Chemical Change (Paper 2)

Law: Law of Conservation of Mass

Example: Total mass before and after a reaction is equal.

Common Mistake: Ignoring coefficients when balancing equations.

Practice: Balance: H₂ + O₂ → H₂O

Organic Chemistry (Paper 2)

Concept: Homologous series

Example: Alkanes follow the formula CₙH₂ₙ₊₂.

Common Mistake: Forgetting functional groups.

Practice: Name the functional group of alcohols.
