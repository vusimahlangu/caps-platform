Chapter 6: Algebra

Introduction

Algebra in Grade 12 is about more than just solving for \( x \). It focuses on understanding the Nature of Roots, solving Simultaneous Equations, and mastering Quadratic Inequalities. These skills are essential for the Paper 1 examination.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Quadratic Formula: \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \)
  • The Discriminant (\(\Delta\)): \( \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \)
  • Nature of Roots:
    • If \( \Delta > 0 \): Roots are Real and Unequal.
    • If \( \Delta = 0 \): Roots are Real and Equal.
    • If \( \Delta < 0 \): Roots are Non-real (Imaginary).
    • If \( \Delta \) is a perfect square: Roots are Rational.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Solving Quadratic Inequalities
Solve for \( x \): \( x^2 - 5x + 6 > 0 \)
1. Factorise: \( (x-2)(x-3) > 0 \)
2. Critical Values: \( x = 2 \) and \( x = 3 \)
3. Answer: \( x < 2 \) or \( x > 3 \)
Example 2: Simultaneous Equations
Solve for \( x \) and \( y \):
\( y + 2x = 3 \) (Eq 1) and \( y = x^2 - 3x + 3 \) (Eq 2)
Substitute Eq 1 into Eq 2:
\( (3 - 2x) = x^2 - 3x + 3 \)
\( x^2 - x = 0 \Rightarrow x(x-1) = 0 \)
\( x = 0 \) (then \( y = 3 \)) or \( x = 1 \) (then \( y = 1 \))

Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for \( x \): \( 3x^2 - 2x - 5 = 0 \) (Leave in simplest surd form if necessary).
  2. For which values of \( k \) will the equation \( x^2 + kx + 9 = 0 \) have equal roots?
  3. Solve the following inequality: \( -x^2 + 4x + 5 \leq 0 \).

Summary & Conclusions

  • The Discriminant is the key to predicting the behavior of quadratic graphs without sketching them.
  • In Simultaneous Equations, always isolate the simplest variable first.
  • Quadratic Inequalities are best solved by finding critical values and checking the regions on a number line.
