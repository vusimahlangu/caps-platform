Chapter 2: Trigonometry & Identities

Introduction

Trigonometry in Paper 2 focuses on proving relationships between angles. Unlike Grade 11, we now deal with Compound Angles (\(\alpha \pm \beta\)) and Double Angles (\(2\alpha\)). These identities allow us to simplify complex trigonometric expressions and solve equations.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Compound Angles:
    • \(\cos(\alpha - \beta) = \cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta\)
    • \(\sin(\alpha + \beta) = \sin\alpha\cos\beta + \cos\alpha\sin\beta\)
  • Double Angles:
    • \(\sin 2\alpha = 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha\)
    • \(\cos 2\alpha = \cos^2\alpha - \sin^2\alpha = 1 - 2\sin^2\alpha = 2\cos^2\alpha - 1\)
  • The Square Identity: \(\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1\)

Worked Examples

Example 1: Expanding Compound Angles
Simplify: \(\cos(x - 30^\circ)\)
Step: Use the formula \(\cos\alpha\cos\beta + \sin\alpha\sin\beta\).
Result: \(\cos x \cdot \cos 30^\circ + \sin x \cdot \sin 30^\circ = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\cos x + \frac{1}{2}\sin x\).
Example 2: Using Double Angles to Solve
Solve for \(x\): \(\sin 2x = \cos x\) for \(x \in [0^\circ; 90^\circ]\).
1. \(2\sin x \cos x - \cos x = 0\)
2. \(\cos x(2\sin x - 1) = 0\)
3. \(\cos x = 0\) (\(x=90^\circ\)) or \(\sin x = \frac{1}{2}\) (\(x=30^\circ\)).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Prove that \(\frac{\sin 2\theta}{1 + \cos 2\theta} = \tan \theta\).
  2. If \(\cos 25^\circ = k\), express \(\sin 50^\circ\) in terms of \(k\).
  3. Solve for \(\theta\): \(3\cos\theta = \sin(\theta + 30^\circ)\).

Summary & Conclusions

  • Always look for Double Angle patterns first to simplify fractions.
  • In proofs, start with the More Complex Side and work towards the simpler side.
  • The Sign in compound angle formulas for \(\cos\) is always opposite to the sign in the bracket.
