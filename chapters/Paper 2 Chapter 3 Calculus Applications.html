Chapter 3: Calculus Applications

Introduction

Calculus Applications in Paper 2 focus on Optimization. We use derivatives to find the dimensions of shapes that provide the maximum volume or minimum cost. It is about applying the rule: at a Maximum or Minimum point, the derivative is zero (\( f'(x) = 0 \)).

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Optimizing: To find a max/min value, find the first derivative and set it to 0.
  • Volume of a Rectangular Prism: \( V = L \times W \times H \)
  • Surface Area of a Cylinder: \( SA = 2\pi r^2 + 2\pi rh \)
  • Rate of Change: The derivative represents the instantaneous rate at which one variable changes with respect to another (e.g., \( \frac{dV}{dt} \)).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Maximizing Area
A farmer has 40m of fencing to make a rectangular pen against a wall. Find the max area.
1. Let width be \( x \). Length is \( 40 - 2x \).
2. Area \( A(x) = x(40 - 2x) = 40x - 2x^2 \).
3. \( A'(x) = 40 - 4x = 0 \Rightarrow x = 10 \).
4. Max Area: \( 10(20) = 200m^2 \).
Example 2: Rate of Change
Water is being pumped into a tank such that \( V(t) = 5t^2 + 2t \). Find the rate after 3 seconds.
1. \( V'(t) = 10t + 2 \).
2. \( V'(3) = 10(3) + 2 = 32 \) liters/sec.

Chapter Exercises

  1. A ball is thrown upward. Height is \( h(t) = 20t - 5t^2 \). Find the maximum height reached.
  2. The total cost of producing \( q \) units is \( C(q) = 0.1q^2 + 50q + 1000 \). Find the marginal cost at \( q = 100 \).
  3. Design a cylinder with a volume of \( 500cm^3 \) that uses the minimum surface area. (Find the radius \( r \)).

Summary & Conclusions

  • Optimization always involves setting the derivative to zero.
  • Ensure you have a single variable in your formula before differentiating.
  • Physical constraints (like lengths being positive) determine the Domain of your application.
