Introduction

Calculus in Paper 2 focuses on **differentiation, integration, and applications**. Learners apply these concepts to solve real-world problems, including rates of change, motion, and areas under curves.

Differentiation

Differentiation allows us to find the gradient of a curve at any point.

Rules to Remember:

  • Power rule: d/dx[xⁿ] = n xⁿ⁻¹
  • Sum/difference: derivative term by term
  • Constant multiple: d/dx[c f(x)] = c f'(x)

Worked Example 1

Find dy/dx for y = 3x³ − 5x² + 2x − 7

  1. Power rule term by term:
  2. d/dx[3x³] = 9x²
  3. d/dx[−5x²] = −10x
  4. d/dx[2x] = 2
  5. d/dx[−7] = 0
  6. Answer: dy/dx = 9x² − 10x + 2

Integration

Integration is the reverse of differentiation. It allows us to find areas under curves.

Rules to Remember:

  • ∫ xⁿ dx = xⁿ⁺¹ / (n+1) + C, n ≠ −1
  • ∫ k f(x) dx = k ∫ f(x) dx

Worked Example 2

Find ∫(6x² − 4x + 5) dx

  1. ∫6x² dx = 2x³
  2. ∫−4x dx = −2x²
  3. ∫5 dx = 5x
  4. Include constant: + C
  5. Answer: 2x³ − 2x² + 5x + C

Applications of Calculus

1. Gradient at a Point

Find the slope of y = x² − 2x + 3 at x = 2

  1. Differentiate: dy/dx = 2x − 2
  2. Substitute x = 2 → dy/dx = 2(2) − 2 = 2
  3. Slope at point = 2

2. Area Under Curve

Find area under y = x² from x = 0 to x = 3

  1. Integrate: ∫₀³ x² dx = [x³/3]₀³
  2. Compute: (27/3) − (0/3) = 9
  3. Area = 9 units²
