Introduction to Calculus

Calculus is the study of how things change. In Grade 12, we focus on differentiation and integration, with applications in graphs, rates of change, and areas under curves.

Differentiation

  • Derivative of a function f(x) gives the rate of change
  • Basic rules: power rule, sum/difference rule, constant rule
  • Product rule: (uv)' = u'v + uv'
  • Quotient rule: (u/v)' = (u'v − uv')/v²
  • Chain rule: derivative of composite functions
  • Applications: maxima, minima, gradients of curves, rates of change

Integration

  • Integral is the reverse of differentiation
  • Indefinite integrals: ∫f(x)dx = F(x) + C
  • Definite integrals: ∫[a,b] f(x)dx gives area under the curve
  • Basic rules: power rule, sum/difference, constant multiple
  • Applications: area between curves, displacement from velocity

Worked Examples

Example 1: Differentiate f(x) = 3x² + 5x − 2

Solution: f'(x) = 6x + 5

Example 2: Integrate ∫(2x³ − 3x) dx

Solution: ∫(2x³ − 3x) dx = (2/4)x⁴ − (3/2)x² + C = (1/2)x⁴ − (3/2)x² + C

Example 3: Find maximum of f(x) = −x² + 4x + 1

Solution: f'(x) = −2x + 4 → set f'(x)=0 → x = 2, maximum at x=2

Practice Exercises

  2. Find ∫(4x² − x + 6) dx
  3. Find the gradient of f(x) = x² − 6x + 5 at x = 3
  4. Find the area under y = x² from x = 0 to x = 2

Summary / Key Formulas

  • Power rule: d/dx(xⁿ) = nxⁿ⁻¹
  • Sum/difference: d/dx(u ± v) = u' ± v'
  • Product rule: (uv)' = u'v + uv'
  • Quotient rule: (u/v)' = (u'v − uv')/v²
  • Chain rule: d/dx[f(g(x))] = f'(g(x))·g'(x)
  • Indefinite integral: ∫xⁿ dx = xⁿ⁺¹/(n+1) + C
  • Definite integral: ∫[a,b] f(x) dx = F(b) − F(a)
