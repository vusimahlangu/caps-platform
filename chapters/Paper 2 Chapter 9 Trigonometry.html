Introduction

Paper 2 Trigonometry focuses on identities, equations, CAST, special angles, and double-angle formulae. The goal is logical manipulation — not memorisation.

Revision of Basic Ratios

In a right-angled triangle:

  • sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent ÷ hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite ÷ adjacent

The CAST Diagram

CAST helps determine which trigonometric ratios are positive in each quadrant.

  • Quadrant I: All
  • Quadrant II: Sine
  • Quadrant III: Tangent
  • Quadrant IV: Cosine

Special Angles

Exact values must be known for: 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°

Worked Example 1

Find the exact value of cos 60°

Answer: cos 60° = 1/2

Trigonometric Identities

Identities are equations that are always true. You may only manipulate one side at a time.

  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • tan θ = sin θ ÷ cos θ
  • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ

Worked Example 2

Prove: (1 − sin²θ) / cos²θ = 1

  1. Start with LHS only
  2. 1 − sin²θ = cos²θ
  3. cos²θ ÷ cos²θ = 1

Solving Trigonometric Equations

  1. Solve the basic equation
  2. Use CAST to find all solutions
  3. State answers in the given interval

Worked Example 3

Solve: sin θ = 0.5, for 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360°

  1. Reference angle = 30°
  2. Sine positive in Quadrants I and II
  3. θ = 30°, 150°

Double-Angle Formulae

  • sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ
  • cos 2θ = cos²θ − sin²θ
  • tan 2θ = 2 tan θ ÷ (1 − tan²θ)

Worked Example 4

Given sin θ = 1/2 and θ is acute, find sin 2θ.

  1. cos θ = √(1 − sin²θ) = √(3/4) = √3/2
  2. sin 2θ = 2 × (1/2) × (√3/2) = √3/2

Practice Exercises

  1. Find the exact value of sin 45°
  2. Prove an identity using sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  3. Solve: tan θ = 1 for 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360°
  4. Find sin 2θ if cos θ = 1/2 and θ is acute

Summary

  • Understand ratios before applying identities
  • Use CAST for correct signs
  • Manipulate one side only when proving identities
  • Always give solutions in the correct interval
