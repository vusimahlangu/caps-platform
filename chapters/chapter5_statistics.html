Chapter 5: Statistics

Introduction

Statistics involves the collection, organization, and interpretation of data. In Grade 12, we expand on univariate data (mean, standard deviation) to Bivariate Data, where we explore the relationship between two variables using scatter plots and regression lines.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Mean (\(\bar{x}\)): The average, calculated as \(\bar{x} = \frac{\sum x}{n}\).
  • Standard Deviation (\(\sigma\)): A measure of how spread out data is from the mean: \(\sigma = \sqrt{\frac{\sum(x - \bar{x})^2}{n}}\).
  • Least Squares Regression Line: The line of best fit, written as \(\hat{y} = a + bx\).
    • \(a\): y-intercept
    • \(b\): gradient (slope)
  • Correlation Coefficient (\(r\)): Measures the strength and direction of the relationship (\(-1 \le r \le 1\)).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Standard Deviation
Data: 10, 12, 15, 18, 20 (n=5)
1. Mean \(\bar{x} = \frac{10+12+15+18+20}{5} = 15\)
2. Variance \(\sigma^2 = \frac{(10-15)^2 + (12-15)^2 + ...}{5} = \frac{68}{5} = 13.6\)
3. \(\sigma = \sqrt{13.6} \approx 3.69\)
Example 2: Regression Equation
Given a set of bivariate data where a calculator provides:
\(a = 154.60\) and \(b = 77.13\)
The equation is: \(\hat{y} = 154.60 + 77.13x\)

Chapter Exercises

  1. A data set has a mean of 50 and a standard deviation of 5. How many data points fall within one standard deviation of the mean?
  2. Use your calculator to find the regression line for: (2;5), (4;9), (6;13), (8;17).
  3. If \(r = -0.98\), describe the strength and direction of the correlation.

Summary

  • Standard Deviation measures consistency; a low \(\sigma\) means data is close to the mean.
  • The Regression Line (\(\hat{y} = a + bx\)) is used to predict unknown values.
  • Always check for Outliers, as they can significantly shift the mean and the regression line.
