Introduction

Statistics helps us collect, organise, represent and interpret data. Many learners panic because there seem to be many formulas. In this chapter, we focus on understanding what the data is saying before using any calculations.

Types of Data

  • Discrete data: Whole numbers (e.g. number of learners)
  • Continuous data: Can take any value (e.g. height, mass)
  • Raw data: Data not yet organised

Measures of Central Tendency

1. Mean

The mean is the average value.

Formula:

Mean = Sum of values ÷ Number of values

Worked Example 1 (Very Detailed)

Find the mean of: 4, 6, 8, 10, 12

Step 1: Add all values

4 + 6 + 8 + 10 + 12 = 40

Step 2: Count how many values there are

There are 5 values

Step 3: Divide

Mean = 40 ÷ 5 = 8

Answer: Mean = 8

2. Median

The median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

Worked Example 2

Find the median of: 3, 7, 9, 11, 15

Step 1: Arrange data (already arranged)

Step 2: Identify the middle value

Median = 9

3. Mode

The mode is the value that appears most often.

Worked Example 3

Find the mode of: 2, 4, 4, 6, 8

Step 1: Identify repeated values

4 appears twice

Answer: Mode = 4

Measures of Dispersion

Range

The range shows how spread out the data is.

Formula: Range = Highest value − Lowest value

Worked Example 4

Find the range of: 5, 9, 14, 18

Highest = 18

Lowest = 5

Range = 18 − 5 = 13

Quartiles and Five-Number Summary

Quartiles divide data into four equal parts.

  • Minimum
  • Lower Quartile (Q1)
  • Median (Q2)
  • Upper Quartile (Q3)
  • Maximum

Worked Example 5

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14

Step 1: Median (middle) = 8

Step 2: Lower half = 2, 4, 6 → Q1 = 4

Step 3: Upper half = 10, 12, 14 → Q3 = 12

Box-and-Whisker Plots

These graphs show the spread of data using the five-number summary.

Always label:

  • Minimum
  • Q1
  • Median
  • Q3
  • Maximum

Cumulative Frequency

Cumulative frequency shows how data accumulates. It is used to find medians and quartiles graphically.

CAPS exams often test interpretation rather than drawing.

Practice Exercises

  1. Find the mean of: 5, 7, 9, 11
  2. Find the median of: 4, 6, 10, 12, 14
  3. Find the mode of: 1, 3, 3, 5, 7
  4. Calculate the range of: 2, 8, 15

Summary

  • Mean is the average
  • Median is the middle value
  • Mode is the most frequent value
  • Range shows spread
  • Quartiles divide data into four parts
