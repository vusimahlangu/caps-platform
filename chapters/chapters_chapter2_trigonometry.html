Introduction

Trigonometry studies the relationship between angles and sides of triangles. Many learners struggle because steps are skipped. This chapter explains every step clearly and slowly.

Trigonometric Ratios

For a right-angled triangle:

  • sin θ = opposite / hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite / adjacent

Extended Trigonometric Ratios

  • cosec θ = 1 / sin θ
  • sec θ = 1 / cos θ
  • cot θ = 1 / tan θ

Special Angles

Learners must know the exact values of trigonometric ratios for 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°.

Trigonometric Identities

Identities are equations that are always true.

  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ
  • 1 + cot²θ = cosec²θ

Worked Example 2: Proving an Identity

Prove: 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ

Step 1: Start with the identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

Step 2: Divide all terms by cos²θ

Step 3: sin²θ / cos²θ + cos²θ / cos²θ = 1 / cos²θ

Step 4: tan²θ + 1 = sec²θ

Identity proven.
