Chapter 9: Vectors

Introduction

A vector is a quantity that has both magnitude (size) and direction. Unlike scalars (like mass or time), vectors describe movements in space, such as velocity or force. In this chapter, we explore how to add vectors and calculate the angle between them.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Magnitude: The length of vector \(\vec{u} = (x; y)\) is \(|\vec{u}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}\).
  • Addition: \(\vec{u} + \vec{v} = (x_1 + x_2; y_1 + y_2)\).
  • The Dot Product: \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = x_1x_2 + y_1y_2\).
  • Angle between Vectors: \(\cos \theta = \frac{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}}{|\vec{u}| |\vec{v}|}\).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Magnitude
Find the magnitude of \(\vec{a} = (3; 4)\).
\(|\vec{a}| = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = 5\) units.
Example 2: Vector Addition
If \(\vec{u} = (1; 2)\) and \(\vec{v} = (4; -1)\), find \(\vec{R} = \vec{u} + \vec{v}\).
\(\vec{R} = (1+4 ; 2-1) = (5; 1)\).
Example 3: Orthogonal Vectors
Show that \(\vec{a} = (2; 3)\) and \(\vec{b} = (-3; 2)\) are perpendicular.
\(\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = (2)(-3) + (3)(2) = -6 + 6 = 0\).
Since the dot product is 0, the angle \(\theta = 90^\circ\).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Find the unit vector in the direction of \(\vec{v} = (8; -6)\).
  2. Calculate the dot product of \(\vec{p} = (2; 5)\) and \(\vec{q} = (-1; 4)\).
  3. Determine the angle between \(\vec{a} = (1; 1)\) and \(\vec{b} = (0; 5)\) to one decimal place.

Summary & Conclusions

  • Vector addition follows the parallelogram law.
  • The Dot Product is a scalar value that helps us find the angle between vectors.
  • If \(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0\), the vectors are perpendicular.
  • If \(\vec{u} = k\vec{v}\), the vectors are parallel.
