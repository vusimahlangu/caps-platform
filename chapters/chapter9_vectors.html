Introduction

Trigonometry often feels difficult because learners try to memorise identities without understanding where they come from. In this chapter, we focus on meaning first, then careful manipulation.

Basic Trigonometric Ratios

In a right-angled triangle:

  • sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse
  • cos θ = adjacent ÷ hypotenuse
  • tan θ = opposite ÷ adjacent

These ratios only apply in right-angled triangles.

Worked Example 1 (Very Slow)

In a right-angled triangle, the opposite side is 3 cm and the hypotenuse is 5 cm. Find sin θ.

Step 1: Identify the ratio

sin θ = opposite ÷ hypotenuse

Step 2: Substitute values

sin θ = 3 ÷ 5

Answer: sin θ = 3/5

The CAST Diagram

The CAST diagram helps us remember which trigonometric ratios are positive in each quadrant.

  • Cosine – Quadrant 4
  • All – Quadrant 1
  • Sine – Quadrant 2
  • Tangent – Quadrant 3

CAPS exams test signs frequently — this diagram is essential.

Special Angles

Learners must know the exact values for: 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, and 90°.

Worked Example 2

Find the exact value of sin 30°.

Step 1: Recall special angle value

sin 30° = 1/2

Answer: 1/2

Trigonometric Identities

Identities are equations that are always true. You are not allowed to change both sides at the same time.

Important identities:

  • sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  • tan θ = sin θ ÷ cos θ
  • 1 + tan²θ = sec²θ

Worked Example 3 (Careful Manipulation)

Prove: (1 − sin²θ) / cos²θ = 1

Step 1: Start with the left-hand side only

(1 − sin²θ) / cos²θ

Step 2: Use identity sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

1 − sin²θ = cos²θ

Step 3: Substitute

cos²θ / cos²θ = 1

Conclusion: LHS = RHS ✔

Solving Trigonometric Equations

Always:

  1. Solve the basic equation
  2. Use CAST for additional solutions
  3. Write answers in the correct interval

Worked Example 4

Solve: sin θ = 0.5 for 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360°

Step 1: Find reference angle

sin θ = 0.5 → θ = 30°

Step 2: Use CAST

Sine is positive in Quadrants 1 and 2

Step 3: Find angles

θ = 30°, 150°

Double Angle Formulae

  • sin 2θ = 2 sin θ cos θ
  • cos 2θ = cos²θ − sin²θ
  • tan 2θ = 2 tan θ ÷ (1 − tan²θ)

Worked Example 5

Find the value of sin 2θ if sin θ = 1/2 and θ is acute.

Step 1: Find cos θ

cos θ = √(1 − sin²θ) = √(1 − 1/4) = √(3/4)

Step 2: Apply formula

sin 2θ = 2 × (1/2) × (√3/2) = √3/2

Practice Exercises

  1. Find cos 60°
  2. Prove an identity using sin²θ + cos²θ = 1
  3. Solve tan θ = 1 for 0° ≤ θ ≤ 360°
  4. Find sin 2θ given sin θ

Summary

  • Understand ratios before formulas
  • Use CAST for signs
  • Manipulate one side only in identities
  • Show all steps clearly
