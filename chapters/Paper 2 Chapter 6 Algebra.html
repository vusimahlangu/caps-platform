Chapter 6: Algebra & Nature of Roots

Introduction

In Paper 2, Algebra is the engine behind geometry. We use the Discriminant (\(\Delta\)) to determine if a line is a tangent to a circle or if two graphs intersect. Mastering the Nature of Roots allows us to solve these spatial problems without always sketching them.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • The Discriminant: \(\Delta = b^2 - 4ac\)
  • Nature of Roots Classifications:
    • \(\Delta < 0\): Non-real roots (Graph does not touch x-axis).
    • \(\Delta = 0\): Real, Rational, and Equal roots (Graph touches x-axis at one point).
    • \(\Delta > 0\): Real and Unequal roots (Graph crosses x-axis twice).
    • If \(\Delta\) is a perfect square: Roots are Rational.
  • Quadratic Inequalities: Solve by finding critical values and using a sign table or a rough sketch.

Worked Examples

Example 1: Analyzing Roots
For which values of \(k\) will \(x^2 - 4x + k = 0\) have non-real roots?
1. Set \(\Delta < 0\): \((-4)^2 - 4(1)(k) < 0\)
2. \(16 - 4k < 0 \Rightarrow 16 < 4k\)
3. Answer: \(k > 4\).
Example 2: Quadratic Inequality
Solve for \(x\): \(x^2 - 2x - 8 \leq 0\).
1. Factorise: \((x-4)(x+2) \leq 0\).
2. Critical values: \(x = 4\) and \(x = -2\).
3. Answer: \(-2 \leq x \leq 4\).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Find the nature of the roots for \(2x^2 + 5x - 3 = 0\).
  2. For what values of \(p\) does \(x^2 + px + p = 0\) have equal roots?
  3. Solve the inequality: \(x^2 > 9\).

Summary & Conclusions

  • The Discriminant identifies the number of intercepts without needing a graph.
  • In Paper 2, use this to prove if a line is a tangent to a circle (where \(\Delta = 0\)).
  • Always check if your final inequality answer matches the direction of the parabola (concave up or down).
