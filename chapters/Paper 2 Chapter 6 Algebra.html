Introduction

Algebra in Paper 2 focuses on **solving equations, factorisation, partial fractions, and inequalities**. Learners apply systematic methods to avoid guessing and ensure correct solutions.

Solving Quadratic Equations

  • Factorisation method
  • Quadratic formula: x = [-b ± √(b² − 4ac)] / 2a
  • Completing the square

Worked Example 1

Solve x² − 5x + 6 = 0

  1. Factorise: (x − 2)(x − 3) = 0
  2. Solutions: x = 2, x = 3

Simultaneous Equations

Two equations with two variables solved by:

  • Substitution method
  • Addition/elimination method

Worked Example 2

Solve: 2x + y = 7 and x − y = 1

  1. From second equation: x − y = 1 → y = x − 1
  2. Substitute in first: 2x + (x − 1) = 7 → 3x − 1 = 7 → 3x = 8 → x = 8/3
  3. Then y = 8/3 − 1 = 5/3

Partial Fractions

Used to simplify rational expressions for integration or solving.

Worked Example 3

Express 3x + 5 / (x² − x − 2) as partial fractions

  1. Factor denominator: x² − x − 2 = (x − 2)(x + 1)
  2. Assume: 3x + 5 / (x − 2)(x + 1) = A / (x − 2) + B / (x + 1)
  3. Multiply through: 3x + 5 = A(x + 1) + B(x − 2)
  4. Equate coefficients: A + B = 3, A − 2B = 5 → A = 4, B = −1
  5. Answer: 4 / (x − 2) − 1 / (x + 1)

Inequalities

Solving algebraic inequalities requires careful sign consideration.

Worked Example 4

Solve 2x − 5 > 1

  1. 2x − 5 > 1 → 2x > 6 → x > 3
  2. Solution: x > 3

Practice Exercises

  1. Solve x² + 3x − 10 = 0
  2. Solve simultaneous equations: x + y = 5, 2x − y = 4
  3. Express (2x + 3)/(x² − x − 6) as partial fractions
  4. Solve inequality: 3x + 7 ≤ 16
  5. Factorise and solve: x² − 9x + 20 = 0

Summary

  • Quadratic equations: factorisation, formula, completing square
  • Simultaneous equations: substitution or elimination
  • Partial fractions simplify rational expressions
  • Inequalities require careful sign consideration
  • Always work step by step and show all workings
