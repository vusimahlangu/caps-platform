Chapter 1: Algebra

1. Introduction

This unit covers Quadratic Equations, Inequalities, and the Nature of Roots ($\Delta$). These are the analytical tools used to solve Paper 1 problem situations.

2. Rules & Formulae

Standard Form: $ax^2 + b^2 + c = 0$. The roots depend on the value of the Discriminant ($\Delta$):

\[ \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \]
  • $\Delta > 0$: Real, Unequal Roots (2 x-intercepts).
  • $\Delta = 0$: Real, Equal Roots (1 x-intercept).
  • $\Delta < 0$: Non-Real Roots (No x-intercepts).

3. Core Examples

  1. Quadratic: Solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x-3)=0$.
  2. Inequality: Solve $x^2 - 9 \leq 0 \implies -3 \leq x \leq 3$.
  3. Nature: For $x^2 + 4x + 10 = 0$, $\Delta = (4)^2 - 4(1)(10) = -24$ (Non-real).

5. Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for $x$: $(2x - 1)(x + 4) = 0$.
  2. Show that the roots of $x^2 + kx + 9 = 0$ are equal if $k = \pm 6$.

6. Summary & Perception

Summary: The Discriminant is the "DNA" of a quadratic equation, predicting outcomes before solving.
