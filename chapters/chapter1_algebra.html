Chapter 1: Algebra

1. Introduction

Algebra is the 25-mark foundation of Paper 1. We solve for unknowns and predict roots using the discriminant $\Delta$.

2. Rules & Formulae

The standard quadratic formula for $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Nature of Roots depends on $\Delta = b^2 - 4ac$.

3. 4 Worked Examples

  1. Solve $x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x-3)=0$.
  2. Solve $2x^2 + x - 5 = 0$ (Correct to 2 decimals).
  3. Inequality: $x^2 - 9 \geq 0 \implies x \leq -3$ or $x \geq 3$.
  4. Surds: $\sqrt{x+5} = x - 1$.

5. Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for $x$: $(2x - 1)(x + 4) = 0$.
  2. Show that the roots of $x^2 + kx + 9 = 0$ are equal if $k = \pm 6$.

6. Summary & Perception

Summary: The discriminant $\Delta$ is the DNA of an equation.
