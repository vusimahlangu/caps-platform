Chapter 1: Algebra

Equations, Inequalities & Nature of Roots (Paper 1)

1. Introduction

Algebra is the fundamental building block of Grade 12 Mathematics. This unit focuses on mastering quadratic equations, inequalities, and Nature of Roots to prepare for complex Functions and Calculus. It accounts for approximately 25 marks in Paper 1.

2. Rules & Formulae

The standard quadratic equation is \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \). When factorisation is not possible, apply the Quadratic Formula:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

The Discriminant (\(\Delta\)):

  • If \(\Delta > 0\): Two Real, Unequal Roots.
  • If \(\Delta = 0\): Two Real, Equal Roots.
  • If \(\Delta < 0\): Non-Real / Imaginary Roots.

3. Core Examples

  1. Quadratic: Solve \( x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x-3)=0 \).
  2. Formula: Solve \( 3x^2 - 2x - 4 = 0 \) to two decimal places.
  3. Inequality: Solve \( x^2 - 9 \geq 0 \).
  4. Simultaneous: Solve \( y = 2x + 1 \) and \( x^2 + y^2 = 25 \).
  5. Surds: Solve \( \sqrt{x+2} = x \).

4. Visual Illustrations

Interaction: Drag the 'q' slider to shift the parabola. When the curve does not touch the x-axis, the roots are Non-Real (\(\Delta < 0\)).

5. Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for \( x \): \( (2x - 1)(x + 4) = 0 \).
  2. Solve for \( x \) and \( y \): \( x + y = 3 \) and \( x^2 + y^2 = 5 \).
  3. Determine the values of \( k \) for which \( x^2 + kx + 9 = 0 \) has equal roots.

6. Summary & Perception

Summary: The discriminant (\(\Delta\)) provides the "Nature" of a system's outcome without the need for full calculation.

Real-Life Principle: In economics, "Nature of Roots" can determine the stability of market equilibrium models.
