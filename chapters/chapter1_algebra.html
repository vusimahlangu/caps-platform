Chapter 1: Algebra

Equations, Inequalities & Nature of Roots

1. Introduction

Algebra is the 25-mark foundation of Paper 1. This unit focuses on mastering the manipulation of quadratic equations, inequalities, and the Nature of Roots (\(\Delta\)).

2. Rules & Formulae

The standard quadratic formula for \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \) is:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

The Discriminant (\(\Delta\)):

  • If \( \Delta > 0 \): Roots are Real and Unequal.
  • If \( \Delta = 0 \): Roots are Real and Equal.
  • If \( \Delta < 0 \): Roots are Non-Real.

3. 5 Core Examples

  1. Quadratic: Solve \( x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x-3)=0 \).
  2. Formula: Solve \( 3x^2 - 2x - 4 = 0 \) (to 2 decimals).
  3. Inequality: Solve \( x^2 - 9 \geq 0 \implies x \leq -3 \text{ or } x \geq 3 \).
  4. Simultaneous: Solve \( y + 2x = 2 \) and \( x^2 + y = 8 \).
  5. Surds: Solve \( \sqrt{x-1} + 3 = x \).

5. Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for \( x \): \( (2x-1)(x+4) = 0 \).
  2. Determine \( k \) if \( x^2 + kx + 9 = 0 \) has equal roots.

6. Summary & Perception

Summary: The discriminant (\(\Delta\)) provides the DNA of an equation, telling us if solutions exist before we solve them.
