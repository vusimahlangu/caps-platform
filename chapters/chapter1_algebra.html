Chapter 1: Algebra

1. Introduction

This unit covers the algebraic skills required to solve Paper 1 equations, focusing on quadratic forms and advanced factorisation.

2. Fundamental Rules

The standard form of a quadratic equation is \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \). If it cannot be factorised, use the Quadratic Formula:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

3. Examples

Example 1: Solve for \( x \) in \( 2x^2 - x - 3 = 0 \).

Solution: Using factorisation \((2x - 3)(x + 1) = 0\), therefore \( x = \frac{3}{2} \) or \( x = -1 \).
