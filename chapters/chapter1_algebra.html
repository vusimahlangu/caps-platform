Chapter 1: Algebra

Equations, Inequalities & Nature of Roots

1. Introduction

Algebra is the foundation of Grade 12 Paper 1. This chapter masters the manipulation of quadratic equations, inequalities, and the Nature of Roots to prepare for complex Functions and Calculus.

2. Rules & Formulae

For any quadratic equation in the form \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \):

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

The Discriminant (\(\Delta\)): Determines the "Nature" of roots.

  • If \(\Delta > 0\): Roots are Real and Unequal.
  • If \(\Delta = 0\): Roots are Real and Equal.
  • If \(\Delta < 0\): Roots are Non-Real (Imaginary).

3. Worked Examples

  1. Quadratic: Solve \(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0 \implies (x-2)(x-3)=0 \).
  2. Formula: Solve \(2x^2 + 4x - 1 = 0\) (to 2 decimal places).
  3. Inequality: Solve \(x^2 - 9 \geq 0 \implies x \leq -3\) or \(x \geq 3\).
  4. Simultaneous: Solve \(y=x+2\) and \(x^2+y^2=10\).
  5. Surds: Solve \(\sqrt{x+2} = x\). (Always test for extraneous roots!)

4. Visual Illustrations

Anchor: Observe how the parabola interacts with the x-axis as you change the 'q' value. This determines the Nature of Roots.

5. Chapter Exercises

  1. Solve for \(x\): \(3x^2 - 11x - 4 = 0\)
  2. Solve for \(x\): \(x^2 < 16\)
  3. Determine the values of \(k\) for which \(x^2 + kx + 4 = 0\) has equal roots.

6. Summary & Real-Life Principles

Summary: The discriminant (\(\Delta\)) allows us to predict a system's outcome without fully solving it.

Perception: In engineering, "Nature of Roots" determines if a structure is stable (\(\Delta \geq 0\)) or will vibrate into failure (\(\Delta < 0\)).
