Introduction

Statistics in Paper 2 focuses on **collecting, summarising, and interpreting data**, including **measures of central tendency, dispersion, and probability distributions**. Learners learn to represent data clearly and make conclusions.

Measures of Central Tendency

  • Mean: sum of all values ÷ number of values
  • Median: middle value when data is arranged in order
  • Mode: most frequently occurring value

Worked Example 1 – Mean

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10

  1. Sum = 2 + 4 + 6 + 8 + 10 = 30
  2. Number of values = 5
  3. Mean = 30 ÷ 5 = 6

Measures of Dispersion

  • Range: largest − smallest
  • Variance: average of squared deviations from the mean
  • Standard deviation: √variance

Worked Example 2 – Standard Deviation

Data: 2, 4, 6, 8, 10

  1. Mean = 6
  2. Squared deviations: (2−6)²=16, (4−6)²=4, (6−6)²=0, (8−6)²=4, (10−6)²=16
  3. Variance = (16+4+0+4+16)/5 = 40/5 = 8
  4. Standard deviation = √8 ≈ 2.83

Data Representation

Common representations include:

  • Frequency tables
  • Bar graphs and histograms
  • Pie charts
  • Box-and-whisker plots

Worked Example 3 – Histogram

Data: Test scores of 10 students: 50, 55, 60, 60, 65, 70, 70, 75, 80, 85

  1. Group data in intervals (50–59, 60–69, 70–79, 80–89)
  2. Count frequency: 50–59:2, 60–69:2, 70–79:3, 80–89:3
  3. Draw bars proportional to frequency

Probability Distributions

Statistics often links with probability for predicting outcomes.

  • Discrete distributions: e.g., rolling a die
  • Continuous distributions: e.g., heights of students

Practice Exercises

  1. Find mean, median, and mode of: 3, 7, 7, 9, 10
  2. Calculate standard deviation for: 5, 10, 15, 20, 25
  3. Draw a histogram for data: 2, 3, 5, 7, 7, 8, 10, 10
  4. Determine range and variance for: 4, 6, 9, 12, 15
  5. Interpret a box-and-whisker plot given data

Summary

  • Central tendency summarises data
  • Dispersion shows spread of data
  • Graphs make data visual and interpretable
  • Probability links statistics to predictions
  • Always show all calculations clearly

Chapter Test

  3. Draw histogram or bar graph from data
