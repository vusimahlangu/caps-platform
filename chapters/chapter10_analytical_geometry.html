Chapter 10: Analytical Geometry

Introduction

Analytical Geometry uses algebraic methods to solve geometric problems on the Cartesian plane. In Grade 12, the focus shifts to the Equation of a Circle and the relationship between circles and Tangents.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Circle Equation (at Origin): \( x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \)
  • General Circle Equation: \( (x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2 \)
    • Center: \( (a ; b) \)
    • Radius: \( r \)
  • Distance Formula: \( d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2} \)
  • Tangent Property: A tangent is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact (\( m_{rad} \times m_{tan} = -1 \)).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Finding the Equation
Find the equation of a circle with center \( M(2; -3) \) and radius 5.
\( (x - 2)^2 + (y - (-3))^2 = 5^2 \)
\( (x - 2)^2 + (y + 3)^2 = 25 \)
Example 2: Symmetry & Completing the Square
Find the center of \( x^2 - 4x + y^2 + 6y = 3 \).
1. \( (x^2 - 4x + 4) + (y^2 + 6y + 9) = 3 + 4 + 9 \)
2. \( (x - 2)^2 + (y + 3)^2 = 16 \)
3. Center is \( (2; -3) \), Radius is 4.
Example 3: Equation of a Tangent
Radius gradient \( m = \frac{1}{2} \). Find tangent gradient.
\( m_{tan} = -2 \) (since \( \frac{1}{2} \times -2 = -1 \)).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Determine the equation of the circle with center \( (-1; 4) \) passing through \( (2; 0) \).
  2. Show that the point \( (3; 4) \) lies on the circle \( x^2 + y^2 = 25 \).
  3. Find the equation of the tangent to the circle \( x^2 + y^2 = 10 \) at the point \( (3; 1) \).

Summary & Conclusions

  • The Radius is the distance from the center to any point on the circumference.
  • If the center is not given, use completing the square to find it.
  • Always remember: Radius \(\perp\) Tangent. This is the "key" to solving tangent equations.
