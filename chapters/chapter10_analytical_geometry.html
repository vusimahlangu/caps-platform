Introduction

Analytical geometry connects algebra and geometry. You can describe shapes with equations and solve geometric problems using coordinates.

Distance Between Two Points

Given points P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂):

Distance formula: d = √((x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²)

Worked Example 1

Find distance between A(1, 2) and B(4, 6)

d = √((4−1)² + (6−2)²) = √(9 + 16) = √25 = 5

Midpoint of a Line Segment

Given points P(x₁, y₁) and Q(x₂, y₂):

Midpoint formula: M = ((x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2)

Worked Example 2

Find midpoint of A(1, 2) and B(4, 6)

M = ((1+4)/2, (2+6)/2) = (5/2, 8/2) = (2.5, 4)

Gradient (Slope) of a Line

m = (y₂ − y₁) / (x₂ − x₁)

Worked Example 3

Find slope of line through A(1, 2) and B(4, 6)

m = (6−2)/(4−1) = 4/3

Equation of a Line

Forms to know:

  • Slope-intercept: y = mx + c
  • Point-slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Worked Example 4

Equation of line through A(1, 2) with slope m = 4/3

y − 2 = 4/3(x − 1)

Simplify: y = 4/3 x + 2 − 4/3 = 4/3 x + 2/3

Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

  • Parallel: same slope
  • Perpendicular: slopes multiply to −1

Worked Example 5

Line parallel to y = 4/3 x + 2 passing through (0,0)

y = 4/3 x

Line perpendicular to y = 4/3 x + 2 passing through (0,0)

Slope m = −3/4 → y = −3/4 x

Equation of a Circle

Standard form: (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r²

Centre: (h, k), Radius: r

Worked Example 6

Circle with centre (2, −1) and radius 3

(x − 2)² + (y + 1)² = 9

Intersection of Lines and Circles

Substitute the line equation into the circle equation and solve the quadratic for x.

Worked Example 7

Line: y = x, Circle: x² + y² = 4

Substitute: x² + x² = 4 → 2x² = 4 → x² = 2 → x = ±√2

y = ±√2 → Intersection points: (√2, √2) and (−√2, −√2)

Practice Exercises

  1. Find distance between (−1, 2) and (3, 4)
  2. Find midpoint of (−2, 5) and (4, −1)
  3. Equation of line through (1,1) with slope −2
  4. Find slope of line perpendicular to y = 3x − 5
  5. Circle with centre (0,0) and radius 5 → write equation
  6. Find intersection points between y = x + 1 and x² + y² = 5

Summary

  • Distance, midpoint, slope are basic tools
  • Line equations: slope-intercept & point-slope
  • Parallel & perpendicular lines have predictable slopes
  • Circle equation shows centre & radius
  • Intersections require substitution & solving quadratics
