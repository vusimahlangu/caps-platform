Introduction

Analytical Geometry links algebra and geometry to study points, lines, and shapes in the coordinate plane. In Paper 2, learners focus on **distance, midpoint, gradient, line equations, and circle equations**.

Distance Between Two Points

Use the distance formula:

d = √[(x₂ − x₁)² + (y₂ − y₁)²]

Worked Example 1

Find distance between A(2, 3) and B(5, 7)

  1. d = √[(5 − 2)² + (7 − 3)²] = √[3² + 4²] = √[9 + 16] = √25 = 5

Midpoint of a Line Segment

Use the midpoint formula:

M = ((x₁ + x₂)/2 , (y₁ + y₂)/2)

Worked Example 2

Find midpoint of A(2, 3) and B(5, 7)

  1. M = ((2+5)/2 , (3+7)/2) = (7/2 , 10/2) = (3.5 , 5)

Gradient of a Line

m = (y₂ − y₁)/(x₂ − x₁)

Worked Example 3

Find gradient of line through A(2, 3) and B(5, 7)

  1. m = (7 − 3)/(5 − 2) = 4/3

Equation of a Line

Forms to use:

  • Slope-intercept: y = mx + c
  • Point-slope: y − y₁ = m(x − x₁)

Worked Example 4

Find equation of line through A(2, 3) with gradient 4/3

  1. y − 3 = 4/3(x − 2)
  2. y − 3 = 4/3x − 8/3 → y = 4/3x − 8/3 + 9/3 → y = 4/3x + 1/3

Equation of a Circle

Standard form: (x − h)² + (y − k)² = r², where (h, k) is the centre and r is the radius

Worked Example 5

Circle with centre (2, −1) and radius 3

  1. Equation: (x − 2)² + (y + 1)² = 9

Practice Exercises

  1. Find distance between P(1, 2) and Q(4, 6)
  2. Find midpoint of P(1, 2) and Q(4, 6)
  3. Calculate gradient of line through P(3, 5) and Q(6, 11)
  4. Find equation of line through (1,2) with slope 3
  5. Write equation of a circle with centre (0,0) and radius 5

Summary

  • Distance = √[(x₂−x₁)² + (y₂−y₁)²]
  • Midpoint = ((x₁+x₂)/2 , (y₁+y₂)/2)
  • Gradient = (y₂−y₁)/(x₂−x₁)
  • Equation of line: point-slope or slope-intercept form
  • Circle: (x−h)² + (y−k)² = r²
