Chapter 7: Analytical Geometry (Circles)

Introduction

Analytical Geometry in Grade 12 extends the coordinate geometry of lines to Circles. We analyze circles centered at the origin and those shifted to any point \((a; b)\). The critical skill is understanding the relationship between the circle's radius and its Tangent.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Circle Equation: \((x - a)^2 + (y - b)^2 = r^2\) (Center at \(a;b\)).
  • Distance Formula: \(d = \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2 + (y_2-y_1)^2}\) (Used to find the radius).
  • Midpoint Formula: \(M\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2} ; \frac{y_1+y_2}{2}\right)\) (Used to find the center).
  • Tangent-Radius Theorem: Radius is perpendicular to the tangent line (\(m_{radius} \times m_{tangent} = -1\)).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Equation of a Circle
Find the equation of a circle with center \(C(-3; 1)\) and radius 4.
Step: Substitute into general form.
Result: \((x + 3)^2 + (y - 1)^2 = 16\).
Example 2: Finding Gradient of Tangent
A circle has center \((0; 0)\) and a point \(P(3; 4)\) on the circumference. Find the gradient of the tangent at \(P\).
1. \(m_{radius} = \frac{4-0}{3-0} = \frac{4}{3}\).
2. \(m_{tangent} = -\frac{3}{4}\) (Perpendicular).
Example 3: Completing the Square
Find the center of \(x^2 - 6x + y^2 + 4y = 12\).
1. Group terms: \((x^2 - 6x + 9) + (y^2 + 4y + 4) = 12 + 9 + 4\).
2. Result: \((x - 3)^2 + (y + 2)^2 = 25\). Center: \((3; -2)\).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Find the center and radius of \(x^2 + y^2 + 10x - 2y + 10 = 0\).
  2. Determine the equation of the tangent to \(x^2 + y^2 = 25\) at the point \((4; -3)\).
  3. Calculate the length of the tangent from point \(A(8; 6)\) to the circle \(x^2 + y^2 = 25\).

Summary & Conclusions

  • Completing the square is the primary method for finding circle centers in exams.
  • The product of gradients (\(m_1 \times m_2 = -1\)) is your best tool for tangent equations.
  • Check if a point is inside, on, or outside a circle by comparing its distance from the center to the radius.
