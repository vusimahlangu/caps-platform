1. Limits and First Principles

Calculus is the study of change. We find the gradient of a curve by making the distance between two points (\( h \)) approach zero.

\[ f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h) - f(x)}{h} \]

2. The Tangent Crawler

The derivative \( f'(x) \) gives us the **gradient** of the tangent at any point \( x \). For \( f(x) = x^2 \), the derivative is \( f'(x) = 2x \).

Interactive: Drag the point T along the curve. Notice how the steepness (gradient) changes depending on your position.

3. Rules of Differentiation

Instead of First Principles, we use rules for efficiency:

  • Power Rule: If \( f(x) = x^n \), then \( f'(x) = nx^{n-1} \)
  • Constant Rule: If \( f(x) = c \), then \( f'(x) = 0 \)
  • Sum Rule: \( [f(x) + g(x)]' = f'(x) + g'(x) \)
