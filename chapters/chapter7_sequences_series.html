Chapter 7: Number Patterns, Sequences & Series

Introduction

Number patterns allow us to predict the future behavior of a sequence. In Grade 12, we study Arithmetic (constant difference), Quadratic (constant second difference), and Geometric (constant ratio) patterns, as well as their sums (Series).

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Arithmetic: \( T_n = a + (n-1)d \) | \( S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a + (n-1)d] \)
  • Geometric: \( T_n = ar^{n-1} \) | \( S_n = \frac{a(r^n - 1)}{r-1} \)
  • Quadratic: \( T_n = an^2 + bn + c \)
    • \( 2a = \text{Second Difference} \)
    • \( 3a + b = \text{First Difference} (T_2 - T_1) \)
    • \( a + b + c = T_1 \)
  • Convergent Series: \( S_\infty = \frac{a}{1-r} \) (if \( -1 < r < 1 \))

Worked Examples

Example 1: Quadratic General Term
Sequence: 4; 7; 12; 19...
1st differences: 3; 5; 7. 2nd difference: 2.
\( 2a = 2 \Rightarrow a = 1 \).
\( 3(1) + b = 3 \Rightarrow b = 0 \).
\( 1 + 0 + c = 4 \Rightarrow c = 3 \).
\( T_n = n^2 + 3 \)
Example 2: Sum to Infinity
Given: \( 8 + 4 + 2 + 1 + ... \)
\( a = 8 \), \( r = 0.5 \). Since \( -1 < 0.5 < 1 \), it converges.
\( S_\infty = \frac{8}{1 - 0.5} = \frac{8}{0.5} = 16 \).
Example 3: Sigma Notation
Evaluate: \( \sum_{k=1}^{5} (2k + 1) \)
Terms: \( 3 + 5 + 7 + 9 + 11 = 35 \).

Illustration: Convergent Geometric Series

Adjust the ratio r. Notice how when \( r \) is between -1 and 1, the terms get smaller and the sum approaches a limit (\( S_\infty \)). If \( r > 1 \), the sequence explodes!

Chapter Exercises

  1. Find the 10th term of the sequence: \( 5; 2; -1; -4 ... \)
  2. Determine \( n \) if \( \sum_{i=1}^{n} (3i - 2) = 155 \).
  3. In a geometric sequence, \( T_3 = 18 \) and \( T_6 = 486 \). Find \( a \) and \( r \).

Summary & Conclusions

  • Linear patterns have a constant difference. Geometric have a constant ratio.
  • A Series is simply the addition of terms in a sequence.
  • The Sigma (\(\Sigma\)) notation is a shorthand way to write a series.
  • Only geometric series with \( |r| < 1 \) have a Sum to Infinity.
