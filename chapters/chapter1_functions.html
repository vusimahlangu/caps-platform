1. Understanding the Gradient \( m \) and Intercept \( c \)

A linear function is defined as \( f(x) = mx + c \). Use the sliders below to see how changing these values tilts and shifts the line.

Interaction: Drag slider m to rotate the line and c to move it up or down.

2. Quadratic Functions: \( f(x) = a(x-p)^2 + q \)

Quadratic functions form a parabola. The vertex is located at \( (p ; q) \).

Interaction: Move p and q to shift the vertex. Change a to flip the graph.

3. The Inverse Reflection

The inverse function \( f^{-1}(x) \) is a reflection of \( f(x) \) across the line \( y = x \). This means coordinates swap: \( (a;b) \rightarrow (b;a) \).

Interaction: Drag the blue point on the curve. Watch how the red point (the inverse) mirrors it across the dotted line \( y = x \).
