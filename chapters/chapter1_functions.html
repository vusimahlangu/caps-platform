Chapter 1: Algebra

Equations, Inequalities & Nature of Roots (Paper 1)

1. Introduction

This chapter revisits quadratic equations and expands into complex inequalities and the Nature of Roots. Algebra is roughly 25 marks of Paper 1.

2. Rules & Formulae

Quadratic Formula: When \( ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \):

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Nature of Roots (Discriminant \(\Delta\)):

  • \(\Delta > 0\): Real, unequal roots.
  • \(\Delta = 0\): Real, equal roots.
  • \(\Delta < 0\): Non-real roots.

3. Five Core Examples

  1. Solve for \(x\): \(x^2 - 5x - 6 = 0\) (Factorisation)
  2. Solve for \(x\): \(3x^2 - 2x - 4 = 0\) (Quadratic Formula)
  3. Inequality: \(x^2 - 4 \leq 0\) (Critical Values)
  4. Simultaneous: \(y + 2x = 2\) and \(x^2 + y = 8\)
  5. Surds: \(\sqrt{x-1} + 3 = x\) (Squaring both sides)
