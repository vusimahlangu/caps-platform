1. Venn Diagrams & The Addition Rule

The Probability of A or B occurring is given by the formula: \[ P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B) \]

Interactive Logic: Drag circles A or B. Watch the intersection area change.
P(A): 0.50
P(B): 0.50
P(A ∩ B): 0.15
P(A ∪ B): 0.85

2. Independent Events

Two events are independent if the outcome of one does not affect the other. Mathematically, they outsmart the "Addition Rule" with the **Product Rule**:

\[ P(A \text{ and } B) = P(A) \times P(B) \]

If this equation holds true, the events are Independent. If not, they are Dependent.

3. Fundamental Counting Principle

This is used to find the total number of outcomes. If there are \( n \) ways to do one thing and \( m \) ways to do another, there are \( n \times m \) ways to do both.

Example (PIN Codes): How many 4-digit PINs are possible if digits can repeat?
\[ 10 \times 10 \times 10 \times 10 = 10,000 \text{ possibilities} \]
If repetition is NOT allowed:
\[ 10 \times 9 \times 8 \times 7 = 5,040 \text{ possibilities} \]
