1. The Unit Circle & Identities

Every point on the unit circle is defined by \( (\cos \theta ; \sin \theta) \). This is the foundation of the identity \( \sin^2 \theta + \cos^2 \theta = 1 \).

Interactive: Drag the point P around the circle. Watch the height (\( \sin \theta \)) and width (\( \cos \theta \)) change.

2. Sketching \( y = a \sin(k(x - p)) + q \)

In Grade 12, we shift and stretch trigonometric graphs. Use the sliders to manipulate the wave.

a (Amplitude): Height of the wave.
k (Period): Frequency of the cycles.
p (Phase Shift): Horizontal movement.
q (Vertical Shift): Up/Down movement.

3. Compound & Double Angle Formulas

The Grade 12 "outsmarting" tool is the use of identities to simplify complex expressions:

  • \( \cos(\alpha \pm \beta) = \cos\alpha\cos\beta \mp \sin\alpha\sin\beta \)
  • \( \sin(2\alpha) = 2\sin\alpha\cos\alpha \)
Note: These are essential for solving 2D and 3D problems in the final exam.
