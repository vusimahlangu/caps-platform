Chapter 4: Probability & Counting

Introduction

Probability in Paper 2 focuses on Counting Principles. We calculate the number of ways objects can be arranged and use these totals to find the probability of specific events occurring. Mastery of factorials (\(n!\)) is the key to outsmarting these problems.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • Fundamental Counting Principle: Total outcomes = \(n \times m \times p \dots\)
  • Factorial Notation: \(n! = n \times (n-1) \times (n-2) \dots \times 1\)
  • Arrangements (Distinct): \(n!\) ways to arrange \(n\) different items.
  • Arrangements (Identical Items): \(\frac{n!}{p! \times q!}\) where \(p\) and \(q\) are counts of identical items.
  • Probability: \(P(E) = \frac{n(E)}{n(S)}\) (favourable outcomes over total outcomes).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Seating Arrangements
7 people sit in a row. How many ways can they be seated if 2 specific people must sit together?
1. Treat the 2 people as 1 "block": Now we have 6 items (5 people + 1 block).
2. Ways to arrange items: \(6!\).
3. Ways to arrange inside the block: \(2!\).
4. Total: \(6! \times 2! = 720 \times 2 = 1440\) ways.
Example 2: Identical Letters
How many unique words can be made from "BANANA"?
Total letters: 6. Identical: 3 'A's, 2 'N's.
Formula: \(\frac{6!}{3! \times 2!} = \frac{720}{6 \times 2} = 60\) ways.

Chapter Exercises

  1. How many 5-digit codes can be formed using digits 0-9 if digits cannot repeat?
  2. In how many ways can 4 different Math books and 3 different Science books be arranged on a shelf if the Science books must stay together?
  3. What is the probability that a random arrangement of the word "APPLE" starts with a vowel?

Summary & Conclusions

  • Use Factorials for total arrangements.
  • When items must be together, treat them as one unit but multiply by their internal arrangements.
  • When items are identical, divide the total factorial by the factorials of the repeated groups.
