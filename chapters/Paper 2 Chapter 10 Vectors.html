Introduction

Vectors describe quantities that have both magnitude and direction. In Paper 2, vectors are used mainly in geometry to describe positions and relationships between points.

Vector Notation

A vector from point A to point B is written as:

AB⃗

If A(x₁, y₁) and B(x₂, y₂), then:

AB⃗ = (x₂ − x₁ , y₂ − y₁)

Worked Example 1: Finding a Vector

Given A(2, 3) and B(6, 7), find AB⃗.

  1. AB⃗ = (6 − 2 , 7 − 3)
  2. AB⃗ = (4 , 4)

Magnitude of a Vector

The magnitude (length) of a vector (x , y) is:

|v⃗| = √(x² + y²)

Worked Example 2

Find the magnitude of the vector (4 , 4).

  1. |v⃗| = √(4² + 4²)
  2. |v⃗| = √(16 + 16)
  3. |v⃗| = √32 = 4√2

Equal and Parallel Vectors

Two vectors are:

  • Equal if they have the same magnitude and direction
  • Parallel if one is a scalar multiple of the other

Worked Example 3

Show that vectors (2 , 3) and (4 , 6) are parallel.

  1. (4 , 6) = 2(2 , 3)
  2. Therefore, the vectors are parallel

Section Formula (Internal Division)

If point P divides the line joining A(x₁, y₁) and B(x₂, y₂) in the ratio m : n, then:

P = ((mx₂ + nx₁)/(m + n) , (my₂ + ny₁)/(m + n))

Worked Example 4

A(2, 4) and B(8, 10). Find the coordinates of P dividing AB in the ratio 1 : 2.

  1. P = ((1×8 + 2×2)/3 , (1×10 + 2×4)/3)
  2. P = (12/3 , 18/3)
  3. P = (4 , 6)

Collinearity of Points

Three points are collinear if:

  • AB⃗ is a scalar multiple of AC⃗, or
  • Their gradients are equal

Worked Example 5

Show that A(1,2), B(3,4), and C(5,6) are collinear.

  1. Gradient AB = (4 − 2)/(3 − 1) = 1
  2. Gradient BC = (6 − 4)/(5 − 3) = 1
  3. Therefore, the points are collinear

Practice Exercises

  1. Find the vector AB⃗ if A(−1,2) and B(3,6)
  2. Calculate the magnitude of (5 , 12)
  3. Show that (3 , 2) and (9 , 6) are parallel
  4. Find the coordinates of a point dividing a line in a given ratio
  5. Determine whether three given points are collinear

Summary

  • Vectors have magnitude and direction
  • AB⃗ = (x₂ − x₁ , y₂ − y₁)
  • Magnitude = √(x² + y²)
  • Parallel vectors are scalar multiples
  • Section formula divides a line in a given ratio
