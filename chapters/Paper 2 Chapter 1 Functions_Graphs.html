Chapter 1: Functions & Graphs

Introduction

In Paper 2, functions are often tested in the context of their Inverses and Asymptotes. Understanding the mapping of coordinates from \( f(x) \) to \( f^{-1}(x) \) is essential for solving complex exam problems.

Core Rules & Formulas

  • The Inverse Rule: To find the inverse, swap \( x \) and \( y \).
  • Notation: \( f^{-1}(x) \) is a reflection of \( f(x) \) about the line \( y = x \).
  • Logarithmic Inverse: If \( f(x) = a^x \), then \( f^{-1}(x) = \log_a x \).
  • Domain Restriction: For \( f(x) = x^2 \) to have an inverse that is a function, we must restrict the domain to \( x \geq 0 \).

Worked Examples

Example 1: Restricting the Domain
Given \( f(x) = 2x^2 \). If the inverse must be a function, what is the domain of \( f \)?
Answer: \( x \geq 0 \) or \( x \leq 0 \).
Example 2: Symmetry
If point \( (2 ; 8) \) lies on \( f(x) = b^x \), which point lies on \( f^{-1} \)?
Answer: Swap coordinates: \( (8 ; 2) \).

Chapter Exercises

  1. Find the inverse of \( f(x) = 3x - 5 \).
  2. Sketch \( y = \log_2 x \) and its inverse on the same axes.
  3. Given \( f(x) = x^2 \), explain why the inverse is not a function unless the domain is restricted.

Summary & Conclusions

  • Reflection about \( y=x \) swaps all \( (x ; y) \) pairs to \( (y ; x) \).
  • Exponential growth is the "undoing" of logarithmic scales.
