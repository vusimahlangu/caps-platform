Chapter 3: Nature of Roots

Predicting the types of solutions using the Discriminant (\(\Delta\)).

1. Introduction

The "Nature" of roots describes what kind of numbers the x-intercepts are (Real, Non-real, Equal, or Rational) without solving the full equation.

2. Rules: The Discriminant (\(\Delta\))

From the quadratic formula \( x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \), the part under the square root is the discriminant:

\[ \Delta = b^2 - 4ac \]
  • \(\Delta < 0\): Non-real / Imaginary roots.
  • \(\Delta \geq 0\): Real roots.
  • \(\Delta = 0\): Real and Equal roots (one x-intercept).
  • \(\Delta > 0\): Real and Unequal roots (two x-intercepts).
  • \(\Delta\) is a Perfect Square: Rational roots.

3. Examples

  1. Determine Nature: For \(x^2 - 4x + 4 = 0\).
    \(\Delta = (-4)^2 - 4(1)(4) = 0\). Nature: Real and Equal.
  2. Unknown Variable: For what values of \(k\) will \(x^2 + kx + 9 = 0\) have equal roots?
    Set \(\Delta = 0 \implies k^2 - 4(1)(9) = 0 \implies k = \pm 6\).

4. Illustration: Touching vs Crossing

Interaction: Drag the 'q' slider. Notice how the graph "lifts" off the x-axis. When it doesn't touch the axis, \(\Delta < 0\) (Non-real).

5. Chapter Exercise

  1. Show that the roots of \(x^2 - (k+1)x + k = 0\) are always real for all real values of \(k\).
  2. Discuss the nature of roots for \(3x^2 - 2x + 1 = 0\).

6. Summary & Perception

Finding: \(\Delta\) is the "DNA" of a quadratic equation.

Perception: In computer graphics (like video games), the nature of roots determines if a projectile (like a ball) will hit a target or miss it entirely.
