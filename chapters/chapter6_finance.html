Introduction

Financial Mathematics deals with money over time. Learners often struggle because they confuse formulas. In this chapter, we slow everything down and focus on understanding what the money is doing.

Simple Interest

Simple interest is calculated on the original amount only.

Formula:

I = P × r × t

  • P = Principal (starting amount)
  • r = Interest rate (as a decimal)
  • t = Time in years

Worked Example 1 (Very Slow)

R5 000 is invested at 8% per annum simple interest for 3 years. Calculate the interest earned.

Step 1: Identify values

P = 5 000

r = 8% = 0.08

t = 3

Step 2: Substitute into formula

I = 5 000 × 0.08 × 3

Step 3: Calculate

I = 1 200

Answer: Interest earned = R1 200

Compound Interest

Compound interest is calculated on the original amount plus previously earned interest.

Formula:

A = P(1 + r)t

Worked Example 2

R4 000 is invested at 10% per annum compound interest for 2 years. Calculate the final amount.

Step 1: Identify values

P = 4 000

r = 0.10

t = 2

Step 2: Substitute

A = 4 000(1 + 0.10)2

Step 3: Calculate exponent first

(1.10)2 = 1.21

Step 4: Multiply

A = 4 000 × 1.21 = 4 840

Answer: Final amount = R4 840

Comparing Simple and Compound Interest

CAPS exams often ask learners to compare the two methods. Compound interest always gives more over time.

Growth

Growth refers to quantities increasing over time (population, bacteria, money).

Formula:

A = P(1 + r)t

Worked Example 3

A population of 20 000 grows at 5% per year. Find the population after 4 years.

Step 1: Identify values

P = 20 000

r = 0.05

t = 4

Step 2: Substitute

A = 20 000(1.05)4

Step 3: Calculate

A ≈ 24 310

Answer: Population ≈ 24 310

Decay

Decay refers to quantities decreasing over time (depreciation, radioactive material).

Formula:

A = P(1 − r)t

Worked Example 4

A car worth R180 000 depreciates at 15% per year. Find its value after 3 years.

Step 1: Identify values

P = 180 000

r = 0.15

t = 3

Step 2: Substitute

A = 180 000(0.85)3

Step 3: Calculate

A ≈ 110 160

Answer: Value ≈ R110 160

Practice Exercises

  1. Calculate simple interest on R6 000 at 7% for 5 years.
  2. Find the final amount for R3 500 at 9% compound interest for 3 years.
  3. A population grows at 4% per year. Find the formula.
  4. A machine depreciates at 12% per year. Write the decay formula.

Summary

  • Simple interest grows linearly
  • Compound interest grows exponentially
  • Growth uses (1 + r)
  • Decay uses (1 − r)
